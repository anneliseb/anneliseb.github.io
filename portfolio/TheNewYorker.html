The New Yorker team had been working on a redesign for many months. They were building a new, fully responsive site and were coordinating with the print creative team, as the redesign was across all media. I was asked to jump in and help the team finish up a few final sections of the website.

At the same time, the research team gathered findings from different consumer marketing beauty reports and studies. From this we were able to understand where Allure stands in relation to consumers/user’s habits and the beauty industry as a whole.

{02} Contributor Pages

I took this information and began to think through who our users are by developing proto-personas. I used demographic and audience information our research team was able to provide from ComScore and created four personas. The personas included descriptions of their personality, location, behaviors, and motivations—from the college student who likes to go out on the weekend to the mom of three whose focus is anti-aging.

{03} Archive

Specific use cases were developed and “blue sky” design ideas were sketched of not only how we could improve the current tool, but what other new products and instances of the tool could be proposed. Example of ideas were new ways of integrating the product finder with content, an app for getting ready in the morning based on the weather, and a interactive kiosk with a partner retail store.
